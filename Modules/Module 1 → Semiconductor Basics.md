# Semiconductor Fundamentals

1. Conductor, Semiconductor, Insulator
- Conductor: Allows current to flow easily. Examples: Copper, Aluminum.
- Semiconductor: Conductivity between conductor and insulator. Examples: Silicon, Germanium.
- Insulator: Opposes current flow. Examples: Rubber, Glass.

2. Valence Band & Conduction Band
- Valence Band: Contains bound electrons.
- Conduction Band: Contains free electrons that participate in current flow.

3. Band Gap (Eg)
- Energy difference between Valence Band and Conduction Band.
- Conductor: ~0 eV
- Germanium: 0.7 eV
- Silicon: 1.1 eV
- Insulator: >3 eV

4. Silicon Atom Structure
- Atomic Number: 14
- Electron Distribution: 2, 8, 4
- Valence Electrons: 4

5. Covalent Bond
- Bond formed by sharing electrons between atoms.
- Silicon forms covalent bonds with neighboring silicon atoms.

6. Crystal Structure of Silicon
- Each silicon atom forms 4 covalent bonds.
- Stable crystal lattice structure is formed.

7. Free Electron
- Electron released when a covalent bond breaks.
- Contributes to current flow.

8. Hole
- Vacancy left by a missing electron.
- Behaves as a positive charge carrier.

9. Electron-Hole Pair (EHP)
- Generated when a covalent bond breaks.
- Produces one free electron and one hole.

10. Intrinsic Semiconductor
- Pure semiconductor without impurities.
- Number of electrons equals number of holes.
- n = p = ni
- Low conductivity.

11. Extrinsic Semiconductor
- Semiconductor with added impurities (doping).
- Higher conductivity than intrinsic semiconductor.

12. N-Type Semiconductor
- Formed by adding pentavalent impurities.
- Examples: Phosphorus, Arsenic, Antimony.
- Donor impurities.
- Majority carriers: Electrons.
- Minority carriers: Holes.

13. P-Type Semiconductor
- Formed by adding trivalent impurities.
- Examples: Boron, Aluminum, Gallium.
- Acceptor impurities.
- Majority carriers: Holes.
- Minority carriers: Electrons.

14. Majority & Minority Carriers
- Majority Carrier: Present in large numbers.
- Minority Carrier: Present in small numbers.
- N-Type: Electrons majority, holes minority.
- P-Type: Holes majority, electrons minority.

15. Drift Current
- Current due to movement of carriers under an electric field.
- Requires external voltage.
- Electrons move toward positive terminal.
- Holes move toward negative terminal.

16. Diffusion Current
- Current due to movement from high concentration to low concentration.
- No external voltage required.
- Important for PN junction formation.

Key Formulas
- n = p = ni (Intrinsic Semiconductor)
- Drift Current -> Due to Electric Field
- Diffusion Current -> Due to Concentration Gradient
