# PN Junction Diode

17. Formation of PN Junction
- Joining of P-Type and N-Type semiconductors.
- Electrons diffuse from N to P.
- Holes diffuse from P to N.
- Recombination occurs.
- Depletion region forms.

18. Depletion Region
- Region without free carriers.
- Contains immobile ions.
- Acts as an insulator.
- Creates electric field.

19. Barrier Potential
- Built-in voltage across depletion region.
- Opposes diffusion.
- Silicon ≈ 0.7V
- Germanium ≈ 0.3V

20. Unbiased PN Junction
- No external voltage.
- Diffusion current = Drift current.
- Net current = 0.

21. Forward Bias
- P to Positive, N to Negative.
- Depletion width decreases.
- Barrier potential decreases.
- Current flows easily.

22. Reverse Bias
- P to Negative, N to Positive.
- Depletion width increases.
- Barrier potential increases.
- Only small reverse current flows.

23. Diode V-I Characteristics
- Forward current rises rapidly after cut-in voltage.
- Silicon cut-in voltage ≈ 0.7V.
- Germanium cut-in voltage ≈ 0.3V.
- Reverse current remains small until breakdown.

24. Breakdown Region
- Reverse current increases suddenly.
- Occurs at breakdown voltage (VBR).

25. Zener Breakdown
- Heavy doping.
- Thin depletion region.
- Low breakdown voltage.
- Mechanism: Tunneling.

26. Avalanche Breakdown
- Light doping.
- Wide depletion region.
- High breakdown voltage.
- Mechanism: Impact Ionization.

27. Diode Resistance
- Static Resistance: RDC = V/I
- Dynamic Resistance: rd = ΔV/ΔI
- Forward resistance is low.
- Reverse resistance is high.

28. Load Line Concept
- Used to find operating point (Q-point).
- Equation: ID = (VCC - VD)/R
- Q-point = Intersection of load line and diode characteristic.

29. Diode as a Switch
- Forward Bias → ON
- Reverse Bias → OFF
- Ideal ON resistance = 0Ω
- Ideal OFF resistance = ∞Ω

Important Values
- Silicon: 0.7V
- Germanium: 0.3V

Zener vs Avalanche
- Zener → Heavy Doping, Tunneling
- Avalanche → Light Doping, Impact Ionization
