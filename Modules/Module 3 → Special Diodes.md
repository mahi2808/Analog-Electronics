# Special Diodes

30. Zener Diode
- Operates in reverse breakdown region.
- Heavily doped PN junction.
- Thin depletion region.
- Zener Voltage (VZ) remains nearly constant.
- Used for voltage regulation.

31. LED (Light Emitting Diode)
- Emits light in forward bias.
- Recombination produces photons.
- Process: Electroluminescence.
- Requires current limiting resistor.
- Used in indicators and lighting.

32. Photodiode
- Detects light.
- Usually operated in reverse bias.
- Light creates electron-hole pairs.
- Produces photocurrent.
- Dark current exists without light.

LED vs Photodiode

LED:
- Forward Bias
- Electrical Energy → Light

Photodiode:
- Reverse Bias
- Light → Electrical Current
